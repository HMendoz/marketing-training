Getting to the heart of your customer's worldview

Brainstorm as many of these as you possibly can. What things jump out at you about your customer that you might not have taken into consideration before? How can you use this information to align what you're selling with their worldview? Where's the intersection?

  1. Why are you selling what you're selling?
  2. What lead you to that point?
  3. What belief about the world brought you there?
  4. What belief about yourself brought you there?
  5. What belief about other people brought you there?
  6. What's the relationship between what you're selling, and what your belief about the world is? Can you identify it?
  7. How does it say about you NOW?
  8. What does it say about people who buy it?
  9. What exact thoughts might have gone through their head when deciding to buy what you sell - OR NOt? List their thoughts in your words. In favor of buying, and not in favor of buying.
  10. What does the list of "not in favor of buying" further say about their particular worldview?
  11. How might this affect your sales?
  12. is there something you can do to change it? Does it make sense to change it, or not? For them, or for you?
  13. What exact thing will they say to their best friend on the phone about what they bought from you?
  14. When their best friend replies, finish the sentence: "That's so great, you've always been so _________________________________________"
  15. What exact thing will they say to their mother on the phone about what they bought from you?
  16. When their mother replies, finish the sentence: "That's so great, you've always been so _________________________________________"
  17. What commonalities are there between the responses of the best friend and the mother? Tease them out.
  18. What do they do on Saturday night?
  19. What do they do on Sunday morning?
  20. Do they believe in free chopice? Your customer will make a hundred choices today alone. Do they believe they're in charge of their destiny - or do they believe that some (or something?) else has a guiding hand?
  21. What was their upbringing like?
  22. Their education? (These are the formative influences on a person's worldview. Parents/guardians, community, educators. Consider which worldviews they've BEEN EXPOSED TO, and had impressed upon them, by way of these influences.)
  23. Consider their actions. What kind of worldview would encourage or motivate those actions or lifestyle?
  24. Is therer a God? If so, what is God like and what's the relationship?
  25. If there isn't a God, does it matter?
  26. What is truth and can anyone really know the truth anyway?
  27. Where did the universe come from?
  28. What's the meaning of life?
  29. What does it mean to live a good life?
  30. Does it really matter in the end whether or not I live a good life?
  31. Is there life after death?
  32. Are humans basically just smart apes with superior hygiene and fashion sense-or is there more to us than that?
  33. What do they think about abortion?
  34. Euthanasia?
  35. Same-sex relationships?
  36. Public education?
  37. Economic policy?
  38. Foreign aid?
  39. The use of military force?
  40. Environmentalism?
  41. Animal rights?
  42. Generic enchancement?

Look for the quiet intersections.
