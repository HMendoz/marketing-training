The final edits

Andddddd, we've arrived to week six: The week I've been waiting for since the beginning.

The week where we discuss the single most important element that turns average writing into brilliant writing; flat words into emotional ones. Because that's what this is all about, isn't it? Emotion. Helping your words reach out and tap someone on the heart, because this is where real profits lie.

It doesn't matter what you're selling - great brands, great businesses, and people who are able to compel large groups of people shape their messages by the emotional values that differentiate their offerings from the rest—and then use their products to support those values. Not the other way around. The naive person takes a product and then tries to shape an emotional message around it, but what I hope you've learned, if nothing else, is to take the person and shape a message around them. This is how we connect to people.

Thereafter, buying your product is always a byproduct of first buying into your emotional frquency. And so, you've got to set the frequency.

That's precisely how we're going to wrap The Six Appeal Process—with setting the frequency.

Throughout the process, you've slaved to understand who you're talking to, (the most important part of the process), what they're thinking, and how you can use those thoughts to shape your messages.

Now, we're going to talk about how you can further refine and polish those messages so they really hit the right emotional cord. Refine and polish are the two key words here; you've heard me say that writing is more an editing & thinking process than a writing process, and I meant it.

The final step, then, in The Six Appeal Process, is editing at the word level. We want to take what you're saying, and say it better. Once you've got the foundation and psychology pieces in place, this is truly the real trick of writing with emotion.
