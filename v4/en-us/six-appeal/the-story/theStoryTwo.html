The art of pushing buttons

Were you able to draw some absolute truths relating to:

  1. Your industry?
  2. Your customer's problems or need in relation to your industry?
  3. What you're specifically selling them?

Let me ask you something: Was this easy or hard?

If it was easy, good. You're on the road to knowing your target market at the level you need to know them in order to push their buttons. If it was hard, I want to encourage you to go back through the first two lessons and really work on understanding the worldview and the beliefs of the people you're selling to.

Because creating emotional appeal in your writing is precisely about that: Pushing buttons.

And while pushing any old button might be fun, we need to be strategic about which buttons we're (deliberately) pushing, if we want those buttons to actually WORK.

So in order to do that, we've got to know the landscape of our target market's heart. Which buttons on their heart are the ones we want to be pushing, wherein “the ones we want to be pushing” are defined as “the ones that will help them feel a soul-mate level match between them and you.”

Everything I've asked you to do to this point is all pre-work necessary to figure out the right buttons.

Because once you know which buttons to push, we can determine how to use your words to push them.
