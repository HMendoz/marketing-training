Adapting this framework for multiple purposes

Since you can apply The Six Appeal Process to anything you're writing, (with a goal of a sale of some sort), then it follows that I can't give you an exact sentence-by-sentence of what you should write next. That will depend on what the purpose of your writing is.

But what you want to do is take the messaging and the logical supporting points we've laid out, and use these to guide your prose.

Do you have to use every single emotional high point? No.

Do you have to talk about every single logical point under each high point? No

Can you focus on only one for a message? Sure.

How you use this messaging is going to depend on what you're writing, and the perception you want to give your reader.

If you're writing a homepage, maybe you select the most salient, (like the hook), and use that to guide your main homepage message.

If a picture says a thousand words, a canvas says a thousand, "I love yous."

Got plenty of love, but not plenty of time?
We've got your saving grace.

If you're writing a Company About Page, maybe you use some emotional high points and, in between, strategically plant the logical points you've outlined.

Put thoughtfulness an autopilot. (While you sit back and enjoy a Cab Saw.)

You always try to be the thoughtful one. The one who clearly pays attention, who listens to other people speak, and who reaps oodles of brownie points when it's gift-giving season...again.

There's just one problem: Thoughtful takes time. It takes energy. It takes creativity. It takes ideas. (And probably a glue gun.) Right?

Goood news. turns out, you don't have to be a Pinterest goddess to seem like one.

Welcome to Love On Canvas, the print technology company that makes you look good, even when it's hard.

We take your photographs-even the torn, stained ones-and turn them into works of art you'll be proud to give as gifts. (Or keep shamelessly for yourself.)

Every woman is different in the way she loves. And she should be different in the way she gives.

And best of all? Art doesn't have to cost like artwork.

If "love don't cost a thing", then buying a piece of it to put on your wall, (or somebody else's), only costs a smidge more.

But that's okay, because this year going to let the Jones family have the generic Picasso...as they scramble to try and keep up with you. gives.

If you're writing a Personal About Page, maybe you use all of the emotional high points and, in between, tell your story as logical points that support your statements.

"I don't have time" came out of my mouth more often than any good mom should admit.

I didn't mean to ignore them. I didn't mean to always be in a rush. But I did, and I was.

I longed for the time leisurely lounge around making arts and creafts, baking cookies and putting Martha Stewart to shame. (Can you imagine putting Martha Stewart to shame?)

But since thoughtfulness and creativity always took (too much) time, I thought I was destined to be one of those Spaghetti-O moms, haphazardly throwing all our old photos into a shoebox, wrapping'er up with duct tape, 'til death do us part.

that is, until discovered---surprise---you don't always have to be one of those Pinterest goddess to seem like one.

Because, you see, there's this little thing called technology...

...etc.

If you're writing a condensed bio, maybe you figure out a way to combine several of the emotional high point messaging into one or two really hard-hitting sentences that pull it all together.

Love On Canvas puts thoughtfulness on autopilot, helps you look like a crafty, creative Pinterest Goddess (even when you aren't one), and creates photo-inspired canvas art & gifts that will make even the Jones Family? Scramble to keep up with you.

Furthermore, if you're writing a book summary, maybe you use the emotional high points to guide & narrow the frame of the summary. Or if you're writing a cover letter, maybe you use the emotional high points that will appeal to the interviewer / company as an outline and then fill in with your experience in those areas.

The Six Appeal steps are adaptable to help you reach almost any end result, as long as you put thought into your words—and emotional messaging/logical supporting system—from the start.
