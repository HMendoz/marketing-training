Fleshing out supporting content around your message

So we've gone through creating our emotionally drenched Six Appeal statements.

Now, what?

Well, it depends. If you've already got something you've written and you're editing for Six Appeal, then you want to take these statements and work them into your message. On the other hand, if you're starting from scratch, you might use these statements to form the mini-thesis for each paragraph, in the order you listed them, and then flesh out the supporting logical content around the emotional high points.

Our goal here is to create an emotional roller coaster that hits on various different emotional high notes. But hitting emotional high notes isn't enough, because shockingly, we aren't all a bunch of zombies walking around mindlessly just clicking buttons as soon as our pulses rise.

What we need in addition to our high note emotional messaging is supportive, “logical” messaging to mix in, too, so your target has something to feed their brains—not just their hearts. Because while the heart is most important, we all want to feel like we're making smart choices, too. And in order to feel that way, we've got to use facts and logic to support the big picture emotional messages.

There isn't a magical number of supporting points you want to make under each emotional message—I've simply used three here for the sake of ease—but what you DO want to achieve is setting up the whole shebang so the last logical point you're making naturally transitions your reader into the next emotional high. That way, your writing won't feel discombobulated and disjointed.

So let's brainstorm some supporting “logic” points that will support our big, lofty emotional appeal statements. We're going to create the backbone of our message.

For our Love On Canvas example, here are some supporting logic points I might list under each emotional high. (And don't worry about making complete sentences that flow perfectly just yet—just get the ideas down.)

If a picture says a thousand words, a canvas says a thousand, "I love yous." (Hook.)

  1. 1. You love your family.
  2. 2. You don't always know how to express it.
  3. 3. You don't alaways have the time to express it. (You could even do some research and include a fact about working mothers and how they're being pulled in every direction at once, so ultimately, you make her feel better for ther erratic lifestyle, and support her current indentity.)

Put charm, thoughtfulness and creativity on autopilot.

  1. 1. Everyone wants to give a thoughtful gift.
  2. 2. It's hard to have the time to be as thoughtful as you'd like.
  3. 3. You don't need time when you have tools.

Good thing you don't have to be a Pinterest goddess to seem like one.

  1. 1. Technology makes life easy.
  2. 2. Technology gives you hero-like powers.
  3. 3. Technology is here to stay.

Scrapbooks put your love in the attic. We put your love on the wall.

  1. 1. Out of sight, out of mind.
  2. 2. Where you live shouldn't be a sterile house. It should be a home.
  3. 3. Homes have family in them.

Rule #1: Never tell a woman to just "Keep Calm and Carry On."

  1. 1. "Keep Calm and Carry On" is for generic people.
  2. 2. Every mother is different in the way she loves.
  3. 3. Her love should be on display to show it.

Good news! You can (finally) stop pretending to like Picasso.

  1. 1. Be comfortable being who you are---and who your family is.
  2. 2. Home should be your truth.
  3. 3. True love doesn't cost money.

Make the Jone's keep up with you. -How I want to end this with a bang.

Do you see how the last point under each lends itself to flowing right into the next emotional high point? That's intentional. Yes, by golly, you do have to think about this stuff. :)

And remember: There is no (life or death) magic bullet formula. There are no “right answers.” And you have the power to tweak your message as much as you need to over time. But the idea is to give you a strong starting foundation to help you take the thoughts of your ideal customer and client, turn them into the words they need to read, (even if they don't know it), and encourage your odds to get them to take the action you'd like them to. That's copywriting—and why copywriters get paid more than any other writer out there. Because there's a lot that goes into it, and it's not just writing pretty sentences.

Which brings me to our wild card what action you want them to take.
