Reverse engineering

Now, what we want to do is take thoughts, and use them to help us from our messages.

f for any reason you're still struggling, and you don't know your target market well enough to be able to imagine their thoughts, try this: Go into Amazon, look up related products, and study the reviews. Even if you're selling a service or an idea.

Actually, even if you aren't having trouble imagining their thoughts, doing this will provide some excellent intel for you, because we don't want to tell them what we think; we want to tell them what they think.

And in order to do that, we must have a good understanding of how they think.

So if you're selling a product, it's easy enough to hop on Amazon and study some reviews of similar products. You'll find out what people in general think, but what you want to look out for is what the people you are targeting think.

On the other hand, if you're selling a service, look up some books that are related to the service you're selling, even if they aren't an exact match, and read those reviews. Read what people actually SAY. It'll give you a surprising amount of insight into how they think.

So now we want to take those thoughts, and use them to reverse engineer our messaging. How can we reverse engineer those into declarative statements that take a person's thoughts, and position what we're selling as the answer?

Let's return, once again, to our beloved X6 example. If these were the thoughts I imagined my target to have, then how can we translate these into declarative statements that take the thought, and position what we're selling as the answer?

Tought: For all the people from my hometown who called me trailer trash behind my back.

Statement: They might whisper things behind your back. But...wouldn't you?

In this case, we wanto to position the X6 as something along the lines of, "Let's give'em something to talk about."

Tought: Who's laughing now?

Statement: Who's laughing now?

In this case, the thought could be the statement. If you saw a picture of an X6 next to simply the words, “Who's laughing now?” might that have more impact than, “The X6 will make successful a reality”—which would be the typical benefits-driven flatlined statement you'd normally get.

Tought: I knew all my hard work would pay off eventually.

Statement: Hard work isn't a luxury. It's how you're built.

This statement accomplishes two things: Speaks to my worldview and how it influences my identity, and also speaks to the car, and how luxury cars aren't necessarily for fancy people. They're for hard workers like me.

Tought: You really do reap what you sow.

Statement: You made your bed. Now drive it.

This statement speaks to the “reap what you sow” concept, but doesn't come out and directly say “you reap what you sow” because it's a common statement and therefore won't Page 88 of 145 force the brain to pay attention. So I added a little unexpected phrasing here, pairing “You made your bed” with “Now drive it.”

Tought: I might not have the perfect body, but at least I've got this.

Statement: People aren't perfect. But when you own an X6, you don't have to be.

Do you see how this is like a fun matching game? Taking your ideal target reader's thoughts, and translating them into a phrase that's going to hit 'em where it hurts, so to speak. You want their thoughts to influence your words.

Notice that any one of these could also serve as a hook. ;) The idea of this entire step, however, isn't just to refine your hook; it's to develop the foundational pieces of your messaging as a whole that you can weave throughout your regular copy

So before moving on, let's take another example and talk about our lovely super woman who has created the DIY wreath-making kit.

As we discussed prior, the worldview that'll be the most relevant for our super woman to focus on is the worldview of her target market that's going to intersect most with her product.

Something I pointed out prior as being relevant might be, for example, the worldview on what it means to be a successful woman. Naturally, we ideally want to target a reader who is going to support the worldview that being a successful woman means hand made, means quality, means time with your family, means appreciating the little things in life, means slow living.

So when that target is considering buying a DIY wreath making kit, maybe she's thinking:
